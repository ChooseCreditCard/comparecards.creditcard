The Smart Way to Compare Credit Cards

Comparing credit cards is not about points alone. It's about fees, protections, FX charges, travel perks, technology and real-world usability. Here’s how to assess cards objectively.

Visit the Comparison Hub

The Most Important Comparison Factors

Every issuer highlights points and bonuses, but the real differences are in the underlying structure of the card. These factors determine long-term value:

  • FX fees – the real cost of spending abroad
  • Insurance coverage – travel, purchase and protection layers
  • Rewards structure – points vs miles vs cashback
  • Annual fee vs value – what you actually get back
  • Technology – virtual cards, tap-to-pay, mobile wallets
  • Ease of dispute handling and customer support

Common Trade-Offs When Comparing Cards

No card is best at everything. Understanding trade-offs helps you compare properly:

  • High rewards often mean higher annual fees
  • Premium benefits may offset FX fees — but only if you travel
  • Cashback is simple; miles offer more upside but are complex
  • 0% FX cards often reduce insurance or perks
  • Travel perks vary massively between issuers

How to Compare Cards Accurately

A structured approach helps avoid marketing noise:

  1. Define your usage pattern (travel, daily spend, business, student)
  2. Prioritize FX, insurance, rewards or benefits
  3. Compare total annual cost after perks
  4. Check real documentation, not promotional pages
  5. Review the issuer’s app and payment technology
